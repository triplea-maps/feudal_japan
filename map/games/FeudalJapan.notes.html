Feudal Japan



Designed by Rolf Larsson


Japan, in the 16th century, faced one of the longest and bloodiest civil wars in history, called the Sengoku Era or Warring States period.
In this game (inspired by the Shogun/Samurai Swords board game) you control one of 17 historical clans struggling for the domination of Japan.
The map expands the ideas of the board game with: Cavalry, Ships, Objectives and Alliances.
Each clan starts with only his captial and claims more and more territories from his foe.
After a few rounds of easy expanding into passive minor Clan territories, the real fight for domination starts.
Each Clan fights for its own and most of them will be controlled by the AI to make it lively and every game unique.
The AI gets many useful boni in this map and is challenging for a single player match, too.
Defeat your rivals and claim the title of Shogun for your clan! There is much to be conquered!


AI and Settings


The Moore AI provides best results.
Set "AI Pause Duration" to 0 to speed up moves (Game, AI Pause Duration, 0 ms)
Switch off: "Confirm Enemy Casualties" and "Show Battles Between AIs" for more speed,too.
Selecting AIs each time a new game starts, can be dealt with saving before GameSetup Units are selected, rest AI, save game and just load it.
Choose Easy AI for MinorClans.
If a player wants to join while the game has already started, edit the humanmarker for his clan, save the game and next round he is able to play this clan without ai boni.

The following boni can be set for AIs by placing them as the GameSetup player (must be a human):

Alliance Bonus: AI controlled clans are allied at start of the game.
Ashigaru Bonus: AI controlled clans get 1 Ashigaru for free per turn and another one with a chance of 20%.
Betrayer Bonus: AI controlled clans can go to war directly with a human player from an alliance relation, without a warning.
Castle Bonus: AI controlled clans get a castle for free with chance of 20%, per turn.
Daimyo Bonus: AI controlled clans get back 1 Daimyo immediately, if all are lost and has a chance of 25% to get another one back.

Difficulty Settings


Very Easy: No boni for AI in Game Options and do not place a humanmarker.

Easy:No boni for AI in Game Options and place the humanmarker in your capital.

Normal:Give the AI some boni(GameSetup) and place the humanmarker.

Hard:All AI boni(GameSetup) and place the humanmarker.

Harder:A small bonus for AI in Game Options, all AI boni(GameSetup) and place the humanmarker.

Hardest:A bigger bonus from Game Options, all AI boni(GameSetup) and place the humanmarker.
It is easy to take out each clan one by one, so play with only one ally or non.
Do only one political action per turn or non.
Think about a harder starting position( play as Asai or Miyoshi).

Increase the AI boni in Game Options for even more challenge, but do not get angry if you get defeated easily by AI.

Minor Clans


The MinorClans are not meant to be played by a human.
MinorClans place some castles at start of the game randomly to increase variety.
MinorClans produce one unit(Ronin) per territory and some castles up to a maximum of 14 units per territory, but never attack.

Politics


There are 3 political relationships: War, Neutral and Allied.
From War to Allied or Allied to War, 1 round of Neutral is required.
Be careful, your AI ally may betray you and go to war with you directly from an alliance if you have selected Betrayer Bonus for them.
When you are asked about Peace or Alliance or been war declared on, check the bottom right corner, to see who does this.
Allies can enter each others territories.

Production, Daimyos & Castles


You can produce in every territory, production is limited only by its value.
The maximum number of castles per territory is also determined by its value. Castles do not count for placement. One castle can be placed per turn per territory.
Take care of your Daimyos! You start with 3 of them, but you can not build new ones and they are the best units in the game.
As long as you are in control of your capital, you can buy 1 Daimyo back, if your last one dies.
Your capital may get lost and you will be unable to recruit new units, but you are not out of the game, if you can take it back immediately.

Victory Conditions


Each Capital ( those with the flags ) is a Victory City.
  • 2 Players : Total Victory (You have to hold 10 Capitals at the end of the round)

  • 3 Players : Honorable Victory (You have to hold 8 Capitals at the end of the round)

  • 4 Players : Triggered Victory (You have to hold 7 Capitals)

  • 5+ Players : Projection of Power (You have to hold 6 Capitals at the end of the round)

Support


Supports can stack, Daimyo+Castle+Unit can make an Ashigaru have 5 defense or 3 in an attack (Daimyo+Unit).
Cavalry does not get a defensive bonus from castles and horses never get a bonus.

Additional Information


This game is inspired by the Shogun (Samurai Swords) board game, which is part of the legendary Gamemaster Series from MB (Shogun-Samurai Swords,Axis and Allies,Conquest of the Empire,Fortress America, Broadsides and Boarding Parties).
Simple firearms called Tetsuhos were known since mid of the 13th century, due to Japan's proximity to China. However, warfare changed dramatically in the mid of the 16th century, when better firearms came to Japan by European traders.
They were called Tanegashima or Teppo because they first came to the island of Tanegashima. Within a few years quality and production number of these guns overtook those of every European country at this time.
They were even fireable in the rain (Europeans never figured out) and some Daimyos had more guns than the whole British army.
The time of the Warring states ended with the unification of Japan under Tokugawa Ieyasu at the beginning of the 17th century.
